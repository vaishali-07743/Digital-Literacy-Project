## 📌 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money through digital payment apps. In many cases, the fraudster pretends to be a bank official, customer care executive, or even a known contact.

For example, a college student may receive a call claiming there is an issue with their bank account. The caller asks the student to verify their details and sends a payment request or a suspicious link. The student is instructed to approve the request to “resolve” the issue. Without realizing the risk, the student approves it, and money is instantly deducted from their account.

In some cases, fraudsters ask for OTPs or UPI PINs, which gives them direct access to the account. These scams often target students and young users because they frequently use digital payment apps but may not be fully aware of such threats.

The consequences of such fraud include financial loss, mental stress, and misuse of personal information. This highlights the importance of being cautious while handling online transactions and verifying all requests before taking action.
